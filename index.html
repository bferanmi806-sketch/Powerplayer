Empowering Ilocos farming communities to thrive during the dry season through affordable, low-tech hydroponic innovation.

Explore the Pilot Why It Matters

The Dry Season Challenge

In Ilocos, the dry season brings stalled production and financial strain for small-scale farmers. Current alternatives often overlook the local reality.

Water & Heat Stress

Scarcity of water and intense heat during peak dry months make traditional soil farming labor-intensive and results unpredictable.

Prohibitive Costs

Commercial hydroponic systems are often too expensive for small farming communities, typically designed for industrial or urban use.

Knowledge Gap

DIY solutions often struggle without specific nutrient guidance, accessible training, and consistent community-based follow-up support.

Our Approach

We provide a complete ecosystem: the hardware, the medium, and the knowledge.

Phase One: Pilot

The 7-Kit Prototype

We are launching our first pilot with a dedicated community group in Ilocos. By starting with 7 kits and a focus on kangkong (water spinach), we are testing the durability of our materials and the effectiveness of our training documentation.

The Bridge: Research to Reality

Our Vision

SibolAni was born from conversations about the real struggles of dry-season farming in the Philippines. We recognized that while the technology for hydroponics exists, the accessibility does not.

Our vision is to bridge global agricultural research with local, grassroots implementation. We aren't just building kits; we are building seasonal resilience for families who deserve a harvest all year round.
